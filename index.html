Alt Text & SEO 101

Google relies on alt attributes to understand image context. That data powers Universal Search thumbnails and Google Images rankings. When images lack clear alt text, entire traffic streams disappear.

Best Practices We Follow

  • Describe the image’s core meaning in ≤ 125 characters.
  • Include keywords only when they’re genuinely relevant.
  • Mark decorative graphics with alt="".
  • Keep tone consistent with your brand voice.

Why Alt Text Matters More Than Ever in 2025

Search engines can’t “see” pixels—they rely on alt attributes to understand images. Google’s Images best‑practices guide confirms descriptive alt text is a ranking factor for Universal Search thumbnails and Google Images itself.

Accessibility is the second pillar. WCAG 2.2 AA success criterion 1.1.1 requires text alternatives for non‑text content. Missing or generic alt text forces screen readers to announce “image,” creating a poor experience for 285 million visually‑impaired users worldwide.

Legal Exposure Keeps Growing

In 2023, U.S. plaintiffs filed 4 605 ADA web‑accessibility lawsuits, a 41 % increase since 2020. The Department of Justice lists missing alt text as a primary violation, with settlements often reaching five figures.

SEO Upside: 12 % More Image Traffic

A Backlinko study of one million results found that optimized alt attributes can unlock up to 12 % extra organic visits. Images already power 22 % of all web searches—leaving that traffic untapped is costly.
